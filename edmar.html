SDG-3 GOOD HEALTH AND WELL BEING

WHAT IS SDG-3?

The third SDG strives to promote healthy lives and well-being for all by addressing critical global health issues such as maternity and child health, infectious illnesses, noncommunicable diseases, and mental health. It pushes for universal health coverage, which ensures that everyone has access to high-quality care without financial burden. The goal also emphasizes the significance of resilient health systems that can address both current and emerging health problems. Finally, SDG 3 aims to promote longer, healthier lives and minimize health disparities worldwide.

IMPORTANCE OF SDG-3

The third SDG, which focuses on good health and well-being, is crucial because it has a direct impact on people's capacity to lead happy, fruitful lives. People cannot realize their full potential if they do not have access to high-quality healthcare, education, and a healthy diet. This objective highlights the significance of preventing illnesses and fostering mental health, acknowledging that well-being is the capacity to flourish rather than merely the absence of disease. Communities become stronger and more resilient when everyone is healthy. Society can thrive and promote equality and opportunity for everyone when everyone is healthy.

HEALTH IS WEALTH

Adopting a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet, frequent exercise, and abstaining from dangerous behaviors such as smoking, is essential for preventing health difficulties. Regular check-ups and vaccines can help detect health problems early on. Supporting public health programs and educating communities about health hazards enables people to make educated decisions. By combining individual responsibility with community actions, we can drastically reduce preventable diseases and increase general well-being.
